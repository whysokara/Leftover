Privacy Policy

Last updated July 2026

Local by design

Leftover runs entirely on your iPhone. There is no account, no login, and no server — the app doesn't have one. Nothing you do inside Leftover ever leaves your device.

What Leftover accesses

Leftover uses Apple's Photos framework to show you your photos and videos and, only when you confirm, to delete the ones you choose. It never uploads, copies, or transmits your photos anywhere.

Permissions

Photo Library — Leftover asks for full access so it can find duplicates, screenshots, blurry shots, and large videos across your library; all analysis happens on your device. iOS Limited Access is fully supported: grant only selected photos and Leftover works with just those.

Notifications (optional) — one daily reminder, only if you switch it on in Settings, and only on days you haven't cleaned up yet. Nothing else, ever.

What Leftover collects

Nothing. No analytics, no tracking, no third-party SDKs, no advertising identifiers. Reminder settings are stored only in the app's local storage on your phone.

Deletions

Photos you delete go to your iPhone's own Recently Deleted album for 30 days, exactly as if you'd deleted them from the Photos app. Leftover cannot recover or access them after that.
